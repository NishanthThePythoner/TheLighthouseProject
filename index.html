Core Pillars

The Three Pillars of Peer Support

We teach a simple, three-step action framework designed specifically for real-world teenage behaviors and scenarios.

1. Recognise

Spot the subtle signals of distress in friends. Understand changes in daily behaviors, withdrawal, altered communication patterns, and online activity before things escalate.

  • Drastic changes in regular routines
  • Uncharacteristic social media posts
  • Prolonged withdrawal from friend circles

2. Approach

Start low-pressure, supportive conversations. Learn what to say, how to establish a safe space without judging or lecturing, and how to maintain presence when things are awkward.

  • Using non-threatening observations
  • Creating zero-pressure listening spaces
  • Expressing care without force-diagnosing

3. Guide

Help friends connect to appropriate support without taking on the role of a clinical professional. Know your personal boundaries, and safely guide friends toward experts.

  • Understanding the limit of peer aid
  • Identifying warm transitions to professionals
  • Safely handling emergency escalations
Our Belief

A Different Direction for Youth Mental Health

Traditional approaches put the absolute weight of reaching out entirely on the individual already struggling, often categorization-first. The Lighthouse Project focuses on preparing the friends around them.

Traditional Approach The Lighthouse Way

The Lighthouse Model

Empowering the surrounding peer community

Instead of expecting a struggling teenager to seek out institutional support on their own, we equip friends with the confidence, active listening skills, and boundary-awareness to step in and offer immediate emotional first-aid.

  • Focuses on training the peers who are already around them.
  • Replaces immediate categorization with patient, active presence.
  • Prepares teens to connect friends to professional help safely.
