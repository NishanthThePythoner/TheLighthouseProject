The Lighthouse Project 
“Helping people find their way back” 

Motto 
Support, not solve. 

Mission Statement 
To equip teenagers with the awareness skills to recognise when their peers are struggling emotionally and provide them with guidance on right ways to approach and manage such situations. 

Primary Aim 
To build emotionally aware peer communities where addressing poor mental health amongst one another is treated as a major life skill - teenagers are better equipped to recognise distress in others and respond appropriately. 

Our Belief 
This project is built on the belief that the first and most immediate support system for many teenagers is not a professional, institution, or “trusted adult” -  but rather, their friends.
Many current approaches to youth mental health awareness unintentionally create distance between teenagers and support systems by:
●	placing responsibility primarily on the person already struggling,
●	categorising emotional difficulties through labels and diagnoses too early,
●	relying on other support systems that teenagers may realistically hesitate to use.
Instead, this initiative focuses on preparing peers to become more emotionally aware, approachable, and capable of handling such conversations appropriately.
The goal is not to turn teenagers into therapists or replace professional help. 
The goal is to ensure fewer young people feel unseen, isolated, or unsupported in the moments before professional help is ever reached.

What makes us different 
1.	Unlike the general approach, our project focuses on training the people surrounding those struggling with their mental health rather than placing that burden on the individual themselves
2.	Our system is based supporting not solving - we aim to help people gain the appropriate skills to form a reliable support system amongst teenagers rather than diagnosing people with problems 
3.	We cater to realistic teenage behaviour - ensuring that we target the first and most likely support systems (friends) of those in need of help.
